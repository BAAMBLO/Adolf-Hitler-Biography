Adolf Hitler

Biography

Adolf Hitler (April 20, 1889 - April 30, 1945) was the leader of the Nazi Party and served as Chancellor and FÃ¼hrer of Germany from 1933 until his death in 1945. Born in Braunau am Inn, Austria, Hitler rose to power in Germany as head of the National Socialist German Workers' Party. His ideology centered on the concepts of territorial expansion and racial supremacy, leading to aggressive foreign policies and the initiation of World War II with the invasion of Poland in 1939. Hitler's regime was responsible for the Holocaust, the systematic genocide of six million Jews and millions of other victims, marking one of history's darkest chapters.
